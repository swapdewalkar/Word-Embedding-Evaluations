
Notes on datasets and licenses
------------------------------

If using this data in your research please cite the following paper
and the url of the STS website: http://ixa2.si.ehu.eus/stswiki:

   Eneko Agirre, Daniel Cer, Mona Diab, Iñigo Lopez-Gazpio, Lucia
    Specia. Semeval-2017 Task 1: Semantic Textual Similarity
    Multilingual and Crosslingual Focused Evaluation. Proceedings of
    SemEval 2017.

The scores are released under a "Commons Attribution - Share Alike 4.0
International License" http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/

The texts are released under the following licenses:

- SMTnews: news conversation sentence pairs from WMT

- SMTeuroparl: WMT2008 develoment dataset (Europarl section)
  http://www.statmt.org/wmt08/shared-evaluation-task.html

- postediting: EAMT11 dataset - machine translations with human
  judgements and post-editions (Specia, 2011).
  Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0)
  http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/

- OnWN: pairs of sentences where the first comes from Ontonotes
  glosses and the second from WordNet glosses. We are grateful to the
  OntoNotes team for sharing OntoNotes to WordNet mappings (Hovy et
  al. 2006).

- FNWN: The sentences are sense definitions from WordNet and
  FrameNet. Note that some FrameNet definitions involve more than one
  sentence. The WordNet-FrameNet mappings are funded by NSF 11-536 CRI
  planning award for LexLink, Christiane Fellbaum, Collin Baker,
  Martha Palmer and Orin Hargraves, and by NSF award CRI:CI-ADDO-EN
  0855271 for Christiane Fellbaum and Collin Baker. We are grateful
  for their sharing of the data.

- tweet-news: The dataset is a subset of the Linking-Tweets-to-News
  data set (Guo et al., 2013), which consists of 34,888 tweets and
  12,704 news articles.  The tweets are the comments on the news
  articles.  The news sentences are the titles of news articles.

- belief: pairs were collected from the DEFT Committed Belief
  Annotation dataset

- plagiarism: The dataset is based on (Clough and Stevenson, 2011).
  Creative Commons Attribution-Noncommercial-Share Alike 2.0
  UK: England & Wales License
  http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/2.0/uk/

- question-question: check the following license

  Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 Unported (CC BY-SA 3.0)
  http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/
